Get Cultured, Ya Brute!

.

Listening to the Fireside Chat reaffirmed the reasons I joined DBC. My initial educational interests were mainly in science--biology was my favorite class in high school, I majored in neuroscience in college, and taught middle school science after graduating. What I enjoyed the most about science was the process of finding truth and discovering the unknown, with an experimental approach. To me DBC is like an experiment I'm collaboratively running with the rest of my cohort to find deeper truth in myself. This past week I've learned more than I ever have in the course of one week, and it forced me to really step back and evaluate how I learn best, how I can conquer the challenges in front of me, and to keep in mind ways that I can optimize my time and learning going forward. The fact that we get constant feedback, just like a scientist gathering data, will only accelerate the experiment to eventually become the best version of myself. The preliminary results of this experiment, 18 weeks from now, will hopefully end in a job, but the process I hope to ingrain in myself during DBC will help me continue the experiment for the rest of my life. I'm excited, and humbled to give my all to this process and to help others along the way.
